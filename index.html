Stephen Hawking

Theoretical Physicist

"I have noticed even people who claim everything is predestined, and that we can do nothing to change it, look before they cross the road."

Stephen Hawking

Stephen Hawking is a theoretical physicist, author, and one of the most inspiring people alive today. Despite living with a physical disability that has paralyzed nearly every muscle in his body, he has had a brilliant career in theoretical physics and became a best selling popular science author. Stephen Hawking reminds me that no matter how daunting the challenges in our lives we are capable of great things.

Some interesting facts about Stephen Hawking:

  • He is the former Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at the University of Cambridge. This chair was held by Issac Newton in 1663.
  • He speaks using a speech generating computer attached to a single cheek muscle.
  • Hawking's work was the first to combine quantum mechanics and Einstein's theory of general relativity in a meaningful way. This may be a step toward a unified theory of physics.
  • He used quantum theory to show that black holes radiate heat, and therefore can eventually disappear. This phenonemon was named Hawking Radiation after him.
  • He authored A Brief History of Time which was published in 1988. The book became a best seller and has sold over 10 million copies.
